### Token Economics — NIGHT and DUST Explained

Your Chapter 12 describes the dual resource mechanism but doesn't deep-link the economics. These fill that gap:

| Resource | URL | What It Covers |
|----------|-----|---------------|
| **NIGHT Token** (official page) | https://midnight.network/night | What NIGHT is, how it generates DUST, staking mechanics, and the separation of ownership value from transaction cost |
| **Tokenomics Blog Post** | https://midnight.network/blog/the-tokenomics-powering-midnight-network | Deep dive — distribution, rewards, why DUST exists, how the dual resource mechanism solves gas volatility |
| **Tokenomics Whitepaper (PDF)** | https://45047878.fs1.hubspotusercontent-na1.net/hubfs/45047878/Midnight-Tokenomics-And-Incentives-Whitepaper.pdf | The full whitepaper — reserve mechanics, circulating supply caps, treasury allocation |

**The key insight for your readers**: NIGHT is the ownership/staking token (public, transparent). DUST is the *resource* that powers transactions and private computation — it's generated by staking NIGHT, not bought on the open market. This separation means transaction costs stay predictable regardless of NIGHT's market price. Your Ch. 12 explains the *what*; these links explain the *why* and *how*.
